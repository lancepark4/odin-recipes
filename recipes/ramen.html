Ramen

For those late night munchies...

Hungry late at night but don't want to cook up a complicated meal? This simple Shin Ramen recipe upgraded with just a few melt-in-your-mouth slices of char siu pork, crisp Chinese greens, blanched bean sprouts, a boiled egg, deep-fried shallots, and chilli oil will leave you with minimal cleanup and greatly satisfied!

  • 1 packet of Shin Ramen
  • 1 boiled egg
  • 1 bunch bok choy (optional)
  • 3 tbsp bean sprouts
  • 3 slices char siu pork
  • 1 tbsp chilli paste
  • 1 tbsp chilli oil
  • 1 tsp fried shallots
  1. Bring a pot of 550 ml of water to the boil.
  2. Using a small colander, blanch the bok choy for 30 seconds. If not using bok choy, go ahead and skip this step.
  3. Using a small colander, blanch the bean sprouts for 30 seconds.
  4. Add the Nongshim soup powder, to taste (itâ€™s quite hot).
  5. Add the disc of noodles and cook for 4 minutes on a gentle boil.
  6. Tip the stock and noodles carefully into a soup bowl.
  7. Add the bok choy and bean sprouts.
  8. Add the egg and the pork slices.
  9. Add a good dob of chilli paste to the bowl and pour over some chilli oil.
  10. Sprinkle with spring onions and fried shallots.
